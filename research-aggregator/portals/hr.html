    Introduction to Human Resources

    Human Resources (HR) is the organizational function responsible for managing people throughout the employee lifecycle - from recruitment to retirement.

    Core HR Functions

    • Talent Acquisition - Recruiting and hiring employees
    • Onboarding - Integrating new employees into the organization
    • Compensation & Benefits - Managing pay and rewards
    • Performance Management - Evaluating and developing employees
    • Employee Relations - Maintaining positive workplace relationships
    • Learning & Development - Training and career growth
    • HR Compliance - Ensuring legal adherence

    Strategic HR

    Modern HR is strategic, aligning people practices with business objectives. Strategic HRM focuses on:

    • Workforce planning aligned to business strategy
    • Building organizational capabilities
    • Creating competitive advantage through people
    • Data-driven decision making (HR analytics)

    HR as Business Partner

    HR has evolved from administrative function to strategic business partner. Understanding the business is as important as HR expertise.

    Talent Acquisition & Recruitment

    Recruitment is the process of attracting, screening, and selecting qualified candidates for open positions.

    The Recruitment Process

    1. Workforce Planning

    • Identify current and future staffing needs
    • Analyze skills gaps
    • Plan for succession and growth

    2. Job Analysis & Description

    A job description should include:

    • Job title and reporting structure
    • Key responsibilities and duties
    • Required qualifications and skills
    • Compensation range (increasingly required by law)
    • Physical requirements and work environment

    3. Sourcing Candidates

    • Job boards - Indeed, LinkedIn, Glassdoor
    • Employee referrals - Often highest quality hires
    • Social recruiting - LinkedIn, professional networks
    • Campus recruiting - Universities, career fairs
    • Staffing agencies - For specialized or volume hiring
    • Passive candidate outreach - Recruiting employed individuals

    4. Screening & Selection

    • Applicant Tracking System (ATS) - Resume screening software
    • Phone screens - Initial qualification calls
    • Interviews - Behavioral, technical, panel
    • Assessments - Skills tests, personality assessments
    • Background checks - Criminal, education, employment verification

    Interview Best Practices

    Use behavioral interviewing to predict future performance:

    STAR Method Questions
    STAR Framework:
S - Situation: Describe the context
T - Task: What was your responsibility?
A - Action: What did you do?
R - Result: What was the outcome?

Example Questions:
"Tell me about a time you had to manage a difficult employee."
"Describe a situation where you had to meet a tight deadline."
"Give an example of how you handled conflict in your team."

    Illegal Interview Questions

    Never ask about: age, race, religion, national origin, marital/family status, pregnancy, disability (unless job-related), genetic information, or arrest record.

    Offer & Negotiation

    • Prepare competitive offer based on market data
    • Be prepared for negotiation
    • Document offer details in writing
    • Complete background check before start date

    Compensation & Benefits

    Total rewards includes all forms of compensation and benefits provided to employees.

    Compensation Components

    Base Pay

    • Salary - Fixed annual amount for exempt employees
    • Hourly wages - Pay per hour for non-exempt employees
    • Pay grades/bands - Structured pay ranges by level

    Variable Pay

    • Bonuses - Annual, quarterly, spot bonuses
    • Commission - Sales-based compensation
    • Profit sharing - Company performance-based
    • Stock options/RSUs - Equity compensation

    Benefits Administration

    Required Benefits

    • Social Security and Medicare (FICA)
    • Workers' compensation insurance
    • Unemployment insurance
    • FMLA leave (50+ employees)

    Common Voluntary Benefits

    • Health insurance - Medical, dental, vision
    • Retirement plans - 401(k), pension, profit sharing
    • Paid time off - Vacation, sick leave, holidays
    • Life and disability insurance
    • Wellness programs
    • Employee assistance programs (EAP)

    Pay Equity

    Pay equity ensures employees are paid fairly regardless of protected characteristics. Conduct regular pay audits to identify and address disparities.

    Performance Management

    Performance management is the continuous process of setting expectations, developing capabilities, and evaluating results.

    Performance Management Cycle

    1. Goal Setting

    Use SMART goals:

    • Specific - Clear and well-defined
    • Measurable - Quantifiable outcomes
    • Achievable - Realistic given resources
    • Relevant - Aligned with business objectives
    • Time-bound - Clear deadline

    2. Ongoing Feedback

    • Regular 1:1 meetings (weekly/biweekly)
    • Real-time feedback and coaching
    • Document feedback and progress

    3. Formal Reviews

    • Annual reviews - Comprehensive evaluation
    • Mid-year check-ins - Progress assessment
    • 360-degree feedback - Multi-source input

    4. Development Planning

    • Identify strengths and development areas
    • Create individual development plans
    • Provide resources and support

    Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

    A PIP is a formal document addressing performance deficiencies:

    • Specific performance gaps identified
    • Clear expectations and success metrics
    • Timeline for improvement (typically 30-90 days)
    • Support and resources provided
    • Consequences of not meeting expectations

    Documentation

    Document all performance conversations. Good documentation protects both employees and the organization, and supports fair decision-making.

    Employee Relations

    Employee relations focuses on maintaining positive relationships between employers and employees.

    Workplace Policies

    Essential policies for the employee handbook:

    • At-will employment statement
    • Equal employment opportunity
    • Anti-harassment and discrimination
    • Code of conduct
    • Attendance and time off
    • Remote work/hybrid policies
    • Social media guidelines
    • Drug and alcohol policy
    • Safety and security

    Handling Workplace Issues

    Complaint Investigation Process

    1. Receive and document complaint
    2. Determine investigation scope
    3. Interview complainant, accused, witnesses
    4. Gather and review evidence
    5. Make findings and recommendations
    6. Take appropriate action
    7. Document and follow up

    Progressive Discipline

    Progressive discipline escalates consequences for repeated issues:

    1. Verbal warning (documented)
    2. Written warning
    3. Final written warning
    4. Suspension
    5. Termination

    Consistency

    Apply policies consistently to avoid discrimination claims. Document everything and treat similar situations similarly.

    Employment Law Essentials

    HR professionals must understand employment law to protect both the organization and employees.

    Key Federal Employment Laws

    LawCoverageKey Provisions
    Title VII15+ employeesProhibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin
    ADA15+ employeesProhibits disability discrimination; requires reasonable accommodation
    ADEA20+ employeesProhibits age discrimination (40+)
    FLSAMost employersMinimum wage, overtime, child labor
    FMLA50+ employees12 weeks unpaid leave for qualifying reasons
    COBRA20+ employeesContinuation of health coverage
    OSHAMost employersWorkplace safety standards

    Classification: Exempt vs. Non-Exempt

    Exempt employees are not entitled to overtime under FLSA. To be exempt:

    • Salary basis: Paid predetermined amount regardless of hours
    • Salary level: Meet minimum salary threshold ($684/week as of 2024)
    • Duties test: Perform exempt duties (executive, administrative, professional)

    Termination Best Practices

    • Document performance/conduct issues
    • Review for potential discrimination issues
    • Prepare final paycheck per state law
    • Plan logistics (return of property, system access)
    • Conduct exit interview
    • Provide COBRA notice if applicable

    HR Technology & Analytics

    HRIS (Human Resource Information System) and analytics are transforming HR.

    HR Technology Stack

    • HRIS/HCM - Core HR data and processes (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, BambooHR)
    • ATS - Applicant tracking (Greenhouse, Lever, Workable)
    • Payroll - Pay processing (ADP, Gusto, Paychex)
    • Benefits administration - Benefits enrollment and management
    • LMS - Learning management systems
    • Performance management - Goals and reviews (15Five, Lattice)
    • Engagement surveys - Employee feedback (Culture Amp, Peakon)

    HR Analytics

    HR analytics uses data to improve people decisions.

    Key HR Metrics

    • Time to fill - Days from job posting to accepted offer
    • Cost per hire - Total recruiting costs / number of hires
    • Turnover rate - Separations / average headcount
    • Retention rate - Employees who stayed / starting headcount
    • Engagement scores - Survey-based engagement measurement
    • Revenue per employee - Revenue / number of employees
    • Training ROI - Return on training investment
    Common HR Calculations
    Turnover Rate = (Separations / Avg Headcount) √ó 100

Time to Fill = Date of Hire - Date Job Posted
